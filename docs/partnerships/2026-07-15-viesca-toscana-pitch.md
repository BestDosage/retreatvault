# Viesca Toscana × RetreatVault — Partnership Pitch Plan
**Call: Today (2026-07-15) with Head of Sales, Viesca Toscana**

---

## 1. Who you're talking to — know their world

**The property:** Viesca Toscana, Reggello (~30 min from Florence). Renaissance-era estate bought by Salvatore & Wanda Ferragamo in the 1950s. Owned by Ferragamo Holding, **operated by Il Borro** (Relais & Châteaux sister property). 170 acres. Inventory: 16th-century Manor Villa, 6 villas, 20 suites (Borgo di Pian Rinaldi), Rose Garden, Due Torri cottage. Member of Small Luxury Hotels (SLH) and Virtuoso.

**The strategic signal (your biggest lever):** They are mid-pivot into wellness. Spa La Corte opened 2025 (Spa Suite with whirlpool, Turkish bath, sauna), holistic program with yoga/breathwork/sound-healing instructor Debra Kochanczyk, and press coverage says the owners' stated aim is "making Viesca a holistic retreat for body and mind." **They are trying to become exactly what RetreatVault ranks.** You're not selling them traffic — you're offering to install them in the category they're spending capital to enter.

**What a head of sales cares about:**
- US market growth (highest-ADR inbound segment for Tuscany)
- Direct bookings vs. OTA commissions (Booking/Expedia take 15–20%)
- Shoulder-season occupancy (they're running August discounts + a "book 4 nights, 4th free" promo — they have soft periods)
- Brand-safe exposure (Ferragamo family brand = they will want content approval)

**Small but useful:** their own current promo is literally "stay 4 nights, 4th night complimentary." Your 4-night ask maps to a mechanic they already use. Marginal cost of the comp: near zero on unsold nights.

## 2. Your credibility story (2 minutes, numbers only)

- RetreatVault.com: data-driven directory of **9,400+ wellness retreats worldwide**, each scored on the proprietary WRD rating.
- **15,000 visitors/month today, on a trajectory we project at ~85,000/month within 6 months** (say "projection" — don't state it as fact). Audience: American wellness travelers actively comparing and booking retreats — high intent, exactly their target inbound segment.
- 1,600+ editorial reviews published, YouTube video intelligence on hundreds of properties, city/country landing pages across Europe.
- Premium editorial redesign just shipped (site looks the part — send them the link during the call).
- **YouTube series launching in 2 months** — on-location retreat documentaries for the US audience.

---

## 3. The offer (what Viesca gets)

Frame as: **"The exclusive European flagship of RetreatVault"** — a 6-month pilot.

1. **Singular recommendation status.** Viesca becomes the one wellness retreat RetreatVault recommends to Americans traveling to Europe. Not a listing among 9,400 — the endorsed pick.
2. **Sitewide popup/promotion.** A site popup shown to our US audience driving to a dedicated Viesca page (smart-targeted: Europe-intent pages, luxury-tier browsers — quality clicks, not spray).
3. **Dedicated landing page**, built from professional content we shoot on property: photography, film, the estate story, spa, villas, experiences — pointing at their **direct booking engine with tracked links (UTM)**. Zero commission. Every booking is direct revenue at full ADR, vs. 15–20% OTA toll.
4. **Flagship of our YouTube series.** Launching in 2 months; Viesca is Episode 1 — the anchor property the whole series is framed around. Evergreen film asset they can reuse (with license) in their own marketing.
5. **Premium editorial treatment on RetreatVault:** verified official photography (full hero treatment — our design system reserves it for verified-photo partners), in-depth editorial review, top placement on Tuscany/Italy/Europe pages.
6. **Monthly performance report:** clicks, landing-page traffic, booking-link clickthroughs. Full transparency.

**Media-value framing:** dedicated placement + popup across a six-figure-visitor trajectory + a professionally produced destination film + landing page. Sourced separately (production company + media buy), this is a $15–25K package. The ask in exchange is four room-nights.

---

## 4. The ask (say it plainly, once)

> "In exchange, we'd ask Viesca to host our founder for **four nights** to produce all of this content on property — accommodation plus access to the spa, dining, and experiences for filming. We cover our own travel. Everything we produce, you get approval rights on before it goes live."

- **Timing:** within ~6 weeks (mid/late August window — their soft period, and it hits our YouTube launch in 2 months).
- **Content approval rights** — offer this proactively; a Ferragamo-family property will need it. It costs you nothing and defuses their biggest fear.
- **Term:** 6-month exclusive pilot. If the numbers work, renew and formalize (affiliate/flat-fee structure discussed then — don't negotiate money now).

### Negotiation ladder
| Level | You accept |
|---|---|
| Dream | 4 nights, villa/suite, spa + experiences + dining comped, exclusive flagship, license to reuse footage |
| Target | 4 nights suite, spa access for filming, dining partially comped |
| Floor | 3 nights on their "4th night free" mechanic + media rate on extras — take it and deliver so hard they upgrade at renewal |
| Walk-away | They want payment from you AND no comp — politely defer: "let me send you the media kit and we'll revisit" |

## 5. Call structure (30 min)

1. **Rapport + credibility (3 min).** Congratulate them on Spa La Corte and the wellness direction — shows you did homework. One-line RetreatVault intro with the numbers.
2. **Discovery first (5–7 min) — ask before pitching:**
   - "How big is the US market for you today, and where do you want it to be?"
   - "How much of your business comes direct vs. OTA?"
   - "Which months do you most want to fill?"
   - Their answers tell you which benefit to lead with (direct bookings / US growth / shoulder season).
3. **The offer (5 min).** Points 1–6 above, tuned to what they just said.
4. **The ask (1 min).** Four nights, content trip, approval rights. Then stop talking.
5. **Objections (below).**
6. **Close on next steps (2 min):** "I'll send a one-page partnership summary today. Can we pencil dates the week of August 17th?" Get a date commitment or a named decision-maker.

---

## 6. Objection handling

- **"What's your traffic actually worth to us?"** → "15K/month today, high-intent Americans comparing retreats. We're transparent: monthly click and referral reports, UTM-tracked. If it doesn't perform, the pilot ends at 6 months and it cost you four unsold room-nights."
- **"Why should we be exclusive with you?"** → Flip it: "The exclusivity runs the other way — *we're* exclusive to *you*. You can keep every other channel. We recommend only Viesca in Europe."
- **"We need brand/content approval." (Ferragamo/Il Borro)** → "Built in. Nothing publishes without your sign-off. You'll also get a license to reuse the film and photography in your own channels."
- **"Why don't you just pay a media rate like press?"** → "Press gives you one article. We're building a permanent, updated sales channel — landing page, video, top directory placement — and reporting on it monthly. This is a channel partnership, not a press stay."
- **"We need to check with Il Borro management / ownership."** → "Understood. Who should be on the follow-up? I'll send a one-pager you can forward today." (Get the name — head of sales may not be the decision-maker.)
- **"Can you guarantee bookings?"** → Never guarantee. "I can guarantee placement, production quality, and transparent reporting. Bookings follow intent, and our audience is intent."

---

## 7. Do NOT say

- Don't state 85K/month as fact — it's a projection; they can check SimilarWeb.
- Don't promise "#1 on Google" or specific booking volumes.
- Don't discuss affiliate commissions or money on this call — comp-for-content only; monetization talk belongs at renewal.
- Don't trash OTAs or their existing partners (SLH, Virtuoso) — position as *additive* direct channel.
- Don't accept vague "sounds interesting, send us something" without a next step + date.
